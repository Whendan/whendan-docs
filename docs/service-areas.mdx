# Service Areas

Service areas define where your team is allowed to operate. When dispatch assigns work, WhenDan checks whether addresses fall inside one of your configured zones.

Setting these zones correctly helps you:

- Keep drivers within their coverage area
- Prevent out-of-area assignments
- Scale operations with clear geographic boundaries

## Open Service Areas

1. In the dashboard sidebar, go to `Service`.
2. Select `Service Areas`.

On this page, you will see:

- A map of existing zones
- The `Add Service Area` button
- Zone hover/click interaction for existing areas

If no zones exist yet, the map starts empty until you create your first area.

## Create A New Service Area

1. Click `Add Service Area`.
2. Enter a clear `Service Area Name` that dispatch can recognize quickly.

In the editor, you can define coverage in two ways.

## Option 1: Draw Area On The Map

1. Click `Draw Area`.
2. Click on the map to place boundary points.
3. Continue clicking to outline your zone.
4. Double-click to close the polygon.
5. Click `Edit Area` to adjust points and fine-tune the boundary.

Use this method when you need a custom boundary that does not match a standard administrative region.

## Option 2: Upload GeoJSON

1. Click `Upload GeoJSON`.
2. Select a `.geojson` file from your local machine.
3. The map auto-fits to the uploaded geometry.
4. Confirm the shape and update the area name if needed.

GeoJSON is a standard format used by mapping and government systems for geographic boundaries, and is ideal when you already have validated zone data.

## Save The Service Area

After drawing or uploading your boundary:

1. Verify the zone shape on the map.
2. Confirm the `Service Area Name`.
3. Click `Create Service Area`.

The new zone is saved immediately and appears in your Service Areas map for your team.

## Update Or Reset While Editing

The editor also includes utility actions:

- `Edit Area` to modify an existing drawn polygon
- `Clear Area` to remove the current drawn boundary
- `Clear GeoJSON` to remove uploaded reference geometry

If needed, you can clear and redraw before submitting.
